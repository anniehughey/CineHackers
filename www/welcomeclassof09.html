Cornell Cinema is considered one of the best campus film exhibition programs in the country, and we invite you to sample the wide variety of films we offer during Orientation Week. Throughout the academic year Cornell Cinema screens hundreds of recent and classic films in the 35mm Dolby Surround-Sound equipped Willard Straight Hall Theatre and Uris Auditorium—two great places to see films. Guest filmmakers are also invited to present their work and talk to students.

The Orientation week line-up includes newly restored prints of Jean-Luc Godard's Masculine/Feminine and Stanley Kubrick's Dr. Strangelove. These cinematic masterpieces MUST be seen on the big screen to be fully appreciated!

This week only, films are free on weeknights and $3 over the weekend for new students with Cornell ID. Regular undergraduate student prices are $4.75 or just $35 for a 10-admission discount card good for one full year. Tickets and cards can be purchased at the theatres in the evenings and at the Willard Straight Hall Ticket Desk during the day. Monthly calendars can be picked up at RPCC, Trillium, Olin Library, the Willard Straight Hall lobby, the Day Hall Information Center, the Campus Store, and many other locations around campus and at the theatres. For more information, call 255-3522.

All screenings Sunday, August 21, through Thursday, August 25, are in Willard Straight Hall Theatre; screenings on Friday, August 26 and Saturday, August 27, are in both Willard Straight Hall (WSH) Theatre and G-1 Auditorium, Uris Hall. Schedule is subject to change.
