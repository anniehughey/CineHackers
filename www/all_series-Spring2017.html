film series: spring 2017

  • Skin

    A wide range of films in conjunction with the Society for the Humanities, whose 16-17 focal theme is “Skin," including the Ithaca premiere of Raoul Peck's I Am Not Your Negro

  • Deux au Denis

    Her feature debut, Chocolat, based on her childhood in colonial Cameroon, and her masterpiece Beau Travail (based on Billy Budd) in 35mm prints!

  • An Elegant Party

    Our annual benefit party, this year featuring Ernst Lubitsch's stylish silent comedy So This is Paris, with piano accompaniment by Dr. Philip Carli

  • 3 Kubrick Classics

    Reinventing film sci-fi with 2001: A Space Odyssey, fashioning the perfect Cold War satire in Dr. Strangelove, and imagining a perverse dystopian future with A Clockwork Orange

  • Four Films from
    the Near East

    Recent films—from Israel, Iran, Palestine & Tunisia—introduced by faculty from the Dept. of Near Eastern Studies

  • Demagogues

    Charlie Chaplin's brilliant anti-fascist comedy The Great DIctator, the Marx Bros. in Duck Soup, Peter Sellers in Dr. Strangelove, and a rarity, Gabriel Over the White House

  • The Steampunk Aesthetic

    Neo-Victorian futurism is like catnip to this visionary filmmakers: Jean-Pierre Jeunet & Marc Caro, Terry Gilliam, Hayao Miyazaki, and even Walt Disney

  • LGBT Film Fest

    An international collection of stunning films, headlined by the award-winning Moonlight, with a gender-bending Brazilian entry, a sumptuous Japanese romance & more

  • Recently
    Restored Classics

    These 9 films include classics ranging from The Battle of Algiers to Gold Diggers of 1933, the highlight perhaps is the 25th anniversary restoration of Daughters of the Dust

  • Contemporary
    World Cinema

    5 films, 4 Ithaca premieres, from Brazil, Chile, China, France & India, by both long-standing and emerging masters of the craft

  • Jeunet & Caro

    A new digital restoration of Delicatessen, a Valentine’s Day screening of Amélie, and 35mm screenings of The City of Lost Children and Micmacs from these French visionaries

  • Cornell Alums
    Make Movies

    Work by Cornell writers Robert Schenkkan MFA ’77 (All the Way) and Richard Price ’71 (The Night Of, The Wanderers), two of the films produced by visiting guest Scott Ferguson ’82

  • It’s Oscar Season!

    As usual we offer a chance to view all the short subjects (documentary, animation and live action) before the ceremony, followed by a viewing party in the Bear's Den on Feb 26

  • Cinema in Sage Chapel

    Our Heavenly Bodies (1925), a German silent that explores astronomical wonders real & imagined with an original score performed live by Nashville's Coupler

  • Cult Classics

    From the 70s: Parts 1 & 2 of Coppola's Godfather epic; from the 80s: Gilliams phantasmagorical Brazil; and from the 90s Tarantino's breakthrough, Pulp Fiction

  • IthaKid Film Festival

    Every fall & spring Cornell Cinema and the Ithaca Youth Bureau sponsor Saturday screenings for families: just $5 for adults & $4 for kids 12 & under.

  • More Doc Spots

    Includes 3 films on Oscar's shortlist: Camerperson (a cinematographer's memoir), Tower (about the 1969 sniper attack at UT Austin) and Command and Control (a nuclear near-miss)

  • More Film Favorites

    Missed some of the great releases in 2016! Don't fear, you can catch them finally (or again) on our big screen.
