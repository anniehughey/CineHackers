Cornell Cinema is considered one of the best campus film exhibition programs in the country, and we invite you to sample the wide variety of films we offer during Orientation Week. Throughout the academic year Cornell Cinema screens hundreds of recent and classic films in the 35mm Dolby Surround-Sound equipped Willard Straight Hall Theatre and Uris Auditorium—two great places to see films. Guest filmmakers are also invited to present their work and talk to students.

The Orientation week line-up includes several selections from the 2002 Sight and Sound’s Critics’ and Directors’ Top Ten Poll. Sight and Sound, the prestigious British film journal, started conducting its poll of the top films and directors of all time in 1952, and has done a new poll every ten years since then. Cornell Cinema is pleased to present recently restored prints of Orson Welles’s Citizen Kane, Alfred Hitchcock’s Vertigo, Francis Ford Coppola’s The Godfather, Stanley Kubrick’s 2001: A Space Odyssey, F.W. Murnau’s silent masterpiece Sunrise, and Akira Kurosawa’s Seven Samurai. Look for these other Sight and Sound top ten films in Cornell Cinema’s September calendar: Stanley Kubrick’s Dr. Strangelove and David Lean’s Lawrence of Arabia, and later this fall: Stanley Donen’s Singin’ in the Rain. These cinematic masterpieces MUST be seen on the big screen to be fully appreciated!

This week only, films are free on weeknights (8/24 - 8/28) and $3 over the weekend (8/29 - 8/30) for new students with Cornell ID.
